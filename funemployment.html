Funemployment

Apr 17, 2021

After a whole bunch of quarter-life crises, I'm headed to grad school this fall. It's an exciting development that I didn't think was entirely possible this time last year. There were definitely both "push" and "pull" factors involved; I've been drawn towards researching online communities for a while, and I was also becoming increasingly disillusioned with the tech industry. Thanks to some spectacular burnout, I'm planning to leave my job pretty soon and take a few months to just do....whatever I feel like doing, I guess. This is a luxury I'm very lucky to have; our culture and societal setup just don't allow us to take time to just be human without a job or without propelling ourselves toward a job. There're a lot of factors contributing to burnout, but tying our identities to our jobs and tying our jobs to our sense of worth is certainly a big one. I don't have a whole lot of plans for my funemployment, but here are some ideas:

  • Get better at driving
  • Maybe start helping with projects at Northeastern
  • Write some stuff
  • Read all the books I've accumulated and intended to read
  • Go on a few hikes when people aren't hiking (perks of not having a 9-5)
  • Make the perfect chocolate chip oatmeal cookie
  • Discover whether or not I have a circadian rhythm
  • Figure out how to bike in Boston
  • Dye my hair another weird color

I'm not sure if I'll do some of these things, or any of them, but I'm constantly thinking of new things I could try doing. Overall, though, I just want a funemployment period where I leave with a better sense of what matters to me and what makes me happy.
